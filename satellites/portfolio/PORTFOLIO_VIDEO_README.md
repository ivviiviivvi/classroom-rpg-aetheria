## ğŸ“ The Framework Explained

### Narrative Structure: Problem â†’ Insight â†’ Solution â†’ Impact â†’ Why Me

This isn't a typical portfolio video. It's structured like a strategic case study.

**1. Problem (0:30-1:15)**
- Establishes the business problem (40% dropout rate)
- Shows understanding of stakeholder pain points
- Sets up the "why does this matter?" question

**2. Insight (1:15-2:00)**
- Reveals the core realization that led to the solution
- Demonstrates strategic thinking, not just feature building
- Shows HOW you think, not just what you built

**3. Solution (2:00-3:00)**
- Explains the system in plain English
- Uses analogies and metaphors (worlds, quests, oracles)
- Focuses on user experience, not technical implementation

**4. Impact (3:00-3:45)**
- Quantifies outcomes in business terms
- **Critically:** Discusses tradeoffs and constraints
- Shows maturity by acknowledging what was sacrificed

**5. Why Me (3:45-4:30)**
- Demonstrates four key competencies through the project
- Positions you as strategic partner, not just executor
- Clear, confident invitation to connect

## ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips

1. **Test on non-technical friends first** - If they understand and find it compelling, you've succeeded.

2. **Record voiceover in a closet** - Sounds weird, but clothes absorb sound and reduce echo. Works great.

3. **Use keyboard shortcuts** - Learn your video editor's shortcuts to save hours of clicking.

4. **Export multiple versions** - Create 4-minute, 90-second, and 60-second cuts for different platforms.

5. **Caption everything** - 85% of social media video is watched without sound.

6. **Batch your work** - Record all voiceover in one session, create all graphics in another, edit in one focused block.

7. **Use templates** - Don't reinvent the wheel. Canva and Figma have great templates you can customize.

8. **Get feedback early** - Share a rough cut before polishing. Better to catch issues early.

9. **Invest in audio** - Good audio makes mediocre visuals feel professional. Bad audio ruins great visuals.

10. **Ship version 1.0** - Done is better than perfect. You can always iterate based on feedback.
